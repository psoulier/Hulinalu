Icons

Below you'll find the various meanings of the icons found throughout this site. Beaches are tagged with icons based on community feedback.

Lifeguard

The lifeguard icon indicates that the beach is monitored by lifeguards. Parks have specific hours of operation and may not always have a lifeguard present. Before entering the water, check with the lifeguards for current conditions and when in doubt, don't go out.

Parking

The car icon indicates parking facilities at a beach. If this icon is present, the beach has a parking lot specifically for the beach. Beaches with road-side parking, parking in residential areas, or comercial areas not associated with the beach will not have this icon.

Restrooms

When the "restroom" symbol is present, the beach has bathrooms on-site.

Dogs Allowed

The paw icon indicates a dog-friendly beach. Beaches that allow dogs sometimes have rules governing what parts of the beach your furry friends are allowed. As usual, be respectful of others on the beach (not everyone likes dogs), clean up after your pet, and be sure to follow city and county leash laws.

Boat Ramp

Some beaches have boat ramps available for public use and will be shown with the boat symbol. When using boat ramps, be sure to follow all rules and regulations regarding watercraft and check with the organization that manages the beach for access guideilnes.

Camping

Beaches with this icon allow camping. Each beach has different schedules and regulations regarding camping. Be sure to check with the beach park management organization to obtain any necessary permits.

Public Transportation

Beaches with this icon have some form of public transpotation (such as busses or trains) that operates within walking distance of the beach.
